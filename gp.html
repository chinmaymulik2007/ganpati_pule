🏝️ The Legend of Ganpatipule & Lord Ganpati

According to local lore, Lord Ganesha emerged from the earth to bless the devotees. The temple is known for its unique east-facing idol, while most Ganesh idols face north. The deity’s trunk turns to the left, a rare and sacred form of Lord Ganpati.

The temple complex sits right on the edge of the Arabian Sea — you can hear the waves while offering prayers. Surrounded by lush hills and soft white sands, it’s a perfect blend of devotion and natural beauty. The annual “Ganesh Chaturthi” and “Maghi Ganesh Jayanti” draw thousands of pilgrims.

Beyond spirituality, the area offers scenic walks, local Konkani cuisine, and a peaceful retreat away from city chaos. The 350-year-old temple also has a small museum displaying antique idols and artifacts.

✨ Must-See Highlights

Ganpatipule Beach

Crescent-shaped, cleanest beach with soft sand, ideal for sunset walks and photography. Swimming safe, and shallow waters.

Ancient Architecture

Stone-carved walls, wooden sanctum, and intricate carvings. One of the few temples with Konkani-style shikhara.

Coastal Flavours

Fresh seafood, authentic Malvani curry, and ‘Ganpati special modaks’ sold near temple. Don't miss!

📋 Travel Guide

🕍 Temple Timings

  • Morning: 5:00 AM – 12:00 PM
  • Evening: 4:00 PM – 9:00 PM
  • Aarti: 7:30 AM & 7:30 PM (must attend)
  • Open all days, special darshan on festivals.

🗓️ Best Time to Visit

  • October – February: Pleasant weather, clear skies
  • Monsoon (July–Sept): Lush greenery but heavy rains
  • Avoid extreme summers (March–May) – humid
  • Ganesh Chaturthi (Aug/Sep) is grand but crowded.

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Ratnagiri Airport (48km) or Kolhapur (140km).
  • By Rail: Nearest railway – Ratnagiri (32km), well-connected.
  • By Road: MSRTC & private buses from Mumbai, Pune, Ratnagiri. Direct taxis available.
  • Self Drive: Coastal Highway (NH66) scenic route.

🏨 Accommodation

MTDC resort just next to temple, homestays, budget lodges & luxury beach resorts. Booking advisable in season.
